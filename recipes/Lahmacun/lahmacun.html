Lahmacun

Lahmacun is one of the most enjoyable and favorite recipes to eat.

However, it is also possible to prepare it at home just as delicious as the ones we eat outside. Let's make a delicious lahmacun to serve with plenty of sumac onions.

Ingredients

For the filling
  • 2 red peppers/li>
  • 3 pieces green pepper
  • 2 medium size Onion
  • 1 bunch Parsley
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon pepper paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tea glass liquid oil
  • 1/4 tea glass water
  • 500 gram fatty ground beef
For the dough
  • 5 cups flour
  • 2 water glass water
  • 1 dessert spoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar

Recipe Instructions

  1. In a food processor, take all the ingredients for the filling except the spices, tomato paste, oil and minced meat and grind them. Add the parsley at the end.
  2. Add the spices, tomato paste and oil to the mixture you take from the rondo into a large bowl and mix well.
  3. Finally, add the minced meat and knead it well. In order for the lahmacun to be delicious, your mixture should have a viscous consistency. If it is not thick enough, you can add a little water.
  4. While your mixture is resting, start making your dough. Pour the water into a bowl, add the salt and sugar and mix well. Add flour little by little.
  5. Gradually add the flour to the water and knead until you get a medium-hard dough.
  6. When your dough reaches medium hardness, take it on the counter and knead it for at least 5 more minutes.
  7. Roll the dough into a cylinder and divide it into equal pieces. To make lahmacuns of the same size, your dough should weigh the same.
  8. Cover the equal sized pieces with a slightly damp kitchen cloth and leave them to rest for at least 1 hour.
  9. In the meantime, turn on your oven at 250 degrees fan and turn it on top and bottom and heat it well.
  10. Sprinkle some flour on the kitchen counter and start rolling out your meringues one by one. Try to roll out your dough as thin as possible.
  11. Spread the lahmacun mixture on the thinly rolled dough so that it reaches the edges. Make sure that your mixture is juicy. Do not leave the edges empty.
  12. Place the lahmacun on greaseproof paper. Place it with the greaseproof paper on the bottom of the oven, which you have preheated to 250 degrees with a fan.
  13. While cooking, check the bottom of the lahmacun with a kitchen utensil like a shovel, when it is slightly burnt, you can take it out of the oven and place the other prepared lahmacuns in the oven. You can cook each lahmacun for 7-10 minutes depending on your oven performance.
  14. Then remove and serve hot and warm. Bon appetit!
